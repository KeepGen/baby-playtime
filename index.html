Prevents Flat Head Syndrome

Daily tummy time exercises give your baby a break from lying on his back in this way preventing flattening of the back or side of their head

Visual Stimulation

Build visual motor skills such as two-eye coordination, moving eyes side to side and looking upward independent of head movements

Brain Development

Provides physical stimulation when encouraging your baby to use his limbs to reach, pull, push, twist and kick. The neural networks are stimulated to make new connections and grow in complexity

Sensory Integration

Tummy time supports the development of your baby's vestibular skills, which help his brain to determine whether it's the object that he is looking at or whether it is his head that is moving

Motor Skills

The tummy time mat promotes the building of you baby's body muscles including the arms, hands, neck and shoulders

Hand-Eye Coordination

Encourages the development of hand-eye coordination, allowing your infant to reach for and touch colorful toys that are floating inside the mat
